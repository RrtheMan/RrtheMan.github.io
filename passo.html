The Passo

Introduction

I started my web development internship at FAB Company on June 7th, where I had the opportunity to meet the founder. During my time there, I worked on building a website designed to handle customer accounts, orders, and purchases, helping streamline their online operations.

FAB is a start-up business that specializes in textiles, with a focus on sustainability. The company recycles old materials and repurposes them to create new, custom-designed products with a unique look.

The Website

FAB needed a website to represent their business online and provide users with information about their services. The homepage displays all the main options available to users. However, when a user selects an option, they are redirected to the login page to ensure secure access to the platform's features.

The login page provides users with the option to either log in or sign up. To create an account, users can enter their name, username, password, and email address. Once logged in, they gain access to additional features, such as viewing available stock and placing orders through the dedicated order page.

The Account

Users have access to their personal account, where they can update their home address—this information is used by the company to deliver orders. Within their account, users can also view a history of all orders they’ve placed. They have the option to cancel pending orders or wait for the designer to either approve or decline them. If an order is declined, the user must cancel it. However, if the order is approved, the user will be able to proceed with payment through an integrated PayPal system to complete the transaction securely.
